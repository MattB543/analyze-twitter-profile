A screenshot of an article details the "Dartmouth Scar Experiment," where subjects, believing they had facial scars from makeup, reported experiencing discrimination and disdain, despite researchers having secretly removed the non-existent scars. This experiment demonstrates how individuals' own expectations and beliefs profoundly shape their perceived reality and interactions, a phenomenon known as "predictive processing," with further examples provided in Andy Clark's book, *The Experience Machine: How Our Minds Predict and Shape Reality*.
